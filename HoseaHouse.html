These crawls are part of an effort to archive pages as they are created and archive the pages that they refer to. That way, as the pages that are referenced are changed or taken from the web, a link to the version that was live when the page was written will be preserved.

Then the Internet Archive hopes that references to these archived pages will be put in place of a link that would be otherwise be broken, or a companion link to allow people to see what was originally intended by a page's authors.

The goal is to fix all broken links on the web. Crawls of supported "No More 404" sites.

MISSION STATEMENT

The mission of Henry Hosea House is to provide food and nourishment for the bodies and souls of the men, women and children of the Northern Kentucky/Cincinnati area. Our vision is to provide a safe environment in which to satisfy our clients’ needs for daily nutrition, support, healthy guidance and social interaction. This positive atmosphere enhances their capacity for self-help and self-sufficiency.

Tribute to Volunteers

The cornerstone of the Hosea House Soup Kitchen is our volunteers! More than 3,500 individuals volunteer annually. Our volunteers include individuals, high school and college students, employees of area companies, members of civic and community groups, and congregants from area churches. Our volunteers help prepare and serve meals as well as pick up food from various stores. There are not enough ways to thank these amazing people!
